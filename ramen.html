Homemade ramen

pagina principal

This is an excellent example of how a well-made homemade ramen recipe should look, with all its qualities and the characteristic plating of this dish.

Today we bring you a recipe that will transport your palate directly to Japan: homemade ramen. This dish, known for its deep and rich broth, elastic noodles, and a variety of delicious toppings, is a true delight for the senses. Although it seems complicated to prepare, you will be surprised at how simple it can be to make it at home with the right ingredients and a little patience.

Additionally, we will give you some tips and tricks to customize your ramen according to your preferences. Do you prefer a lighter broth or one with a lot of body? Are you a fan of spicy or looking for something milder? No problem! With our recipe, you will learn to adjust the flavors to your liking, creating a dish that will not only be delicious but also unique and special. So get ready, put on your apron, and dive into the wonderful world of homemade ramen. Let's cook!

Ingredients

  • Water.
  • Pork or chicken bones.
  • Large onion.
  • Head of garlic.
  • Fresh ginger.
  • Carrot.
  • Leek.
  • Soy sauce.
  • Salt.

Ingredients for the noodles and toppings

  • Ramen noodles.
  • Eggs.
  • Roasted pork.
  • Bamboo shoots.
  • Nori sheets.
  • Green onions.
  • Sweet corn.
  • Sesame oil.

Preparation

  1. Prepare the ingredients

    2. Wash and rinse the pork or chicken bones with cold water to remove impurities. Cut the onion in half, the carrots into large pieces, and the ginger into slices. Peel and cut the head of garlic in half. Finely chop the green onions. Boil the eggs for 7 minutes, cool them, peel them, and marinate them in a mixture of soy sauce, mirin, and water (in equal parts) for at least 1 hour.

  2. Cook the broth

    3. In a large pot, add the pork or chicken bones, onion, garlic, ginger, carrots, and kombu seaweed (if using). Add 2 liters of water and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer for at least 4 hours. Then, add 100 ml of soy sauce, 50 ml of mirin, and salt to taste. Cook for another 30 minutes. Strain the broth and keep it warm.

  3. Cook the noodles

    4. Boil the ramen noodles according to the package instructions. Once cooked, drain them and rinse them with cold water to stop the cooking. Divide the noodles among the four bowls.

  4. Prepare the pork

    5. If you didn't buy the pork ready, roast a piece of pork belly with soy sauce, sugar, sake, and water over low heat until tender. Then, slice the chashu thinly.

  5. Prepare the toppings

    6. Cut the bamboo shoots into strips, finely chop the green onions, and cut the nori sheets into strips. If desired, cook some sweet corn to add to the ramen.

  6. Assemble the ramen

    7. Pour the hot broth over the noodles in each bowl. Carefully place the marinated eggs cut in half, the slices of chashu, the bamboo shoots, the sweet corn (if using), and the chopped green onions on top of the noodles. Add a nori sheet to each bowl and, if desired, a drizzle of sesame oil.

  7. serve

    8. Serve the homemade ramen in individual bowls. Garnish with chopped green onion and sesame seeds if desired. Enjoy this delicious homemade ramen dish hot
